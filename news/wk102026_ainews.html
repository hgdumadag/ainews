⚡ 30-Second Executive Summary

The week of March 2–9 will be remembered as the moment AI's political, commercial, and labor dimensions collided simultaneously. Anthropic's $200M Pentagon contract collapsed over AI weapons and surveillance guardrails — and OpenAI rushed in with a competing deal that Altman later admitted was "opportunistic and sloppy," triggering user backlash, a top robotics engineer's resignation, and Claude surging to the #1 App Store spot. Meanwhile, OpenAI launched GPT-5.4 with native computer use and ChatGPT embedded directly in Excel with FactSet, Moody's, and S&P Global integrations — a direct assault on enterprise financial workflows. Nvidia's Jensen Huang declared the company's AI lab investments are ending, a signal of strategic realignment. And Anthropic published the first empirical AI labor displacement map, revealing 75% programmer task coverage while showing unemployment effects remain limited — so far.
